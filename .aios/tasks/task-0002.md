# Define the Review and Result envelope contracts

## Objective

Define the immutable Review document contract and the structured worker
Result envelope so the Loop Engine can run the Sprint 0 loop without
inferring any outcome from prose.

## Acceptance Criteria

- A Review document contract specifies storage, ID-to-file resolution,
  required front matter, an immutable lifecycle, and a structured verdict of
  `pass` or `changes_requested`.
- The `last_review` resolution and verdict checks deferred by `aios.task/v1`
  are activated with precise rules.
- The bootstrap review `review-0001` remains valid evidence without being
  rewritten.
- A Result envelope contract specifies the structured outcome every worker
  adapter returns, covering success payloads for each Sprint 0 Role and
  failure semantics that do not consume retries.
- The Loop Engine can derive an Attempt, a Review, or an approval decision
  from a successful Result without parsing prose.
- Neither contract stores worker identity.

## Constraints

- Do not implement the Loop Engine.
- Do not define Project, Role, Assignment, or worker adapter invocation
  formats.
- Do not persist Result envelopes and do not add event history.
- Do not modify `task-0001`, `review-0001`, or the `aios.task/v1`
  specification.
- Do not add fields without a requirement in the current loop.

## Context

`aios.task/v1` (`.aios/tasks/README.md`) defers exactly these two contracts
in its "Deliberately Omitted" section and names them the prerequisites for
implementing the Loop Engine. `review-0001` exists as a provisional
`aios.review/v0` document referenced by `task-0001`. The non-blocking
findings recorded in `review-0001` target the `aios.task/v1` document itself
and are deferred to a future Task.

## Attempts

### Attempt 1

#### Summary

Defined the immutable Review document contract (`aios.review/v1`) in
`.aios/reviews/README.md`: storage and ID-to-file resolution, required front
matter with a structured `pass`/`changes_requested` verdict, immutability
rules, the activation of the `last_review` checks that `aios.task/v1`
deferred, and a bootstrap exemption for `review-0001`. Defined the transient
worker Result envelope (`aios.result/v1`) in `.aios/results/README.md`:
required fields, success payloads for the implementer, reviewer, and approver
Roles, and failure semantics.

#### Verification

Cross-checked both contracts against the `aios.task/v1` lifecycle and
invariants: every state transition consumes exactly the payload its Role's
Result provides, no contract stores worker identity, and failures do not
consume retries. Verified that `task-0001` and `review-0001` pass the newly
activated checks: `last_review` resolves, `task` and `project` match, the
verdict is `pass`, and the evaluated attempt equals `retry.count + 1`.
